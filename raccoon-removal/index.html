Unusual Noises

Hearing strange scratching, scurrying, or thumping sounds coming from your attic, walls, or crawl space, particularly at night, is a strong indicator of wildlife activity. Raccoons are relatively large animals, and their movements are often noticeable. These noises can be quite unsettling and disruptive, especially when they occur during sleeping hours. Pay close attention to the location and nature of the sounds; this information can be helpful to our technicians. The volume and frequency of the sounds may increase as the raccoon settles in and potentially builds a nest. Young raccoons might also make chattering or whining sounds. Don't dismiss these noises as just 'house settling'; investigate or call for professional help. Persistent nocturnal noises are a significant clue that you have unwanted guests. The sounds can vary depending on what the raccoon is doing, whether it's moving around, foraging, or interacting with its environment. The intensity of the noises can also fluctuate. Listen for sounds that seem out of the ordinary for your home.

Raccoons can cause physical damage to gain entry or create comfortable nesting sites. Addressing this damage promptly is essential for preventing future infestations and maintaining the integrity of your property. Disturbed Trash Cans or Outdoor Areas

Raccoons are notorious for raiding trash cans. If you find your garbage overturned, lids removed, and contents scattered, it's a strong sign that raccoons are visiting your property looking for food. They are opportunistic feeders and are attracted to easy food sources. Secure your trash cans with tight-fitting lids or store them in a secure location to deter them. Regular disturbances of your outdoor bins are a classic symptom of raccoon activity in your area. This messy habit can attract other scavengers as well. Raccoons are particularly active at night, so you might find the evidence of their foraging in the morning. Consider investing in animal-proof trash cans if this is a recurring issue. The mess created by overturned garbage can also attract insects and other pests. Don't leave pet food or bird feeders out overnight.

Seeing Raccoons During the Day

While raccoons are primarily nocturnal, seeing one during the day could indicate that it is sick, injured, or lactating and foraging for food for its young.
